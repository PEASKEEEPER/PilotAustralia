Our Process

How a Ferry Flight Works

A transparent, step-by-step process. No surprises — just professional execution from initial contact to final delivery.

01

Initial Enquiry & Quote

Contact us via the form below, phone, or email. Provide your aircraft type, current location, destination, and preferred timeframe. We'll respond with a detailed quote within a few hours.

02

Route Planning & Weather Assessment

Once confirmed, we begin detailed route planning. NOTAMs, airspace restrictions, fuel stops, and weather windows are all assessed. We won't depart into marginal conditions.

03

Pre-Flight Inspection & Handover

Our pilot arrives at your location and conducts a thorough walk-around and document review. Any pre-existing defects are logged. We won't fly an aircraft that isn't airworthy.

04

The Ferry Flight

Flight plans are filed, ATC clearances obtained, and your aircraft is flown professionally to the destination. You'll receive departure and arrival notifications at each stop.

05

Arrival & Handover Report

On arrival, the aircraft is secured and a full written handover report is provided. This includes flight times logged, fuel consumed, any observations, and condition notes.

Safety & Standards

Safety Isn't a Feature.
It's the Foundation.

Every decision we make is governed by one principle: the aircraft, the pilot, and the public must be safe. Always.

CASA Regulatory Compliance

All operations conducted under CASR Parts 61, 91, and relevant regulations. Current licences, medicals, and recency maintained.

Documented Pre-Flight Process

Standardised inspection checklists, fuel log, weight & balance, and risk assessment completed for every ferry flight.

Go/No-Go Weather Discipline

We use conservative weather minimums. If conditions aren't acceptable, we delay. No schedule pressure overrides pilot judgement.

Comprehensive Hull Insurance

We carry flight insurance for every ferry operation. Aircraft owner's existing coverage is also reviewed and considered in our pre-flight briefing.

A ferry pilot is a licensed aviator hired to fly an aircraft from one location to another on behalf of the owner or operator. Unlike a charter pilot who transports passengers, a ferry pilot transports the aircraft itself — often for sale, maintenance, repositioning, or delivery to a new owner. We are your professional stand-in pilot.
